African Elephants

African Elephants are amazing creatures. They are the largest land animals on Earth and normally live in Africa. African Elephants have two species of Elephants, the African Savanna Elephant (also known as the African Bush Elephant) and the African Forest Elephant. Both the African Savanna Elephant and the African Forest Elephant used to be critically endangered but now only the African Forest Elephant is critically endangered while the African Savanna Elephant is endangered

  1. African Elephant Characteristics
    • Large ears that are shaped like Africa's continent
    • Ivory tusks on both the males and females
    • Trunks that are useful for eating, grabbing items, breathing, and scooping up water
  2. African Elephant Facts
    • Both species can live to be 60-70 years old
    • Both species have the longest pregnancies among mammals; 22 months (almost two years)
    • Both species can run up to 24-25 miles per hour
    • Both species have predators including:
      • Humans
      • Lions
      • Hyenas
      • Crocodiles (if the elephants are sick or young)
    • Both species eat around 300 pounds of food and 50 gallons of water each day
    • Both species usually only have one calf per pregnancy but there have been a few occurrences where they have birthed twins
    • African Savanna Elephants wean their calves when they are 6-18 months old while African Forest Elephants wean their calves when they are 6 years old
    • African Savanna Elephants can weigh between 4-7 tons while African Forest Elephants can weigh between 2-5 tons
    • African Savanna Elephants can grow to be 10-13 feet tall while African Forest Elephants can grow to be 8-10 feet tall
    • African Savanna Elephants live in all of sub-Saharan Africa besides dense tropical forests while African Forest Elephants live in dense tropical forests
  3. African Elephant Diet
    • Tree Roots
    • Bark
    • Branches
    • Leaves
    • Grass
    • Seeds
    • Fruit
